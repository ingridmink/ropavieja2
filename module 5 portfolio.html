Why is Ropa Vieja my favorite comfort meal?

I chose Ropa Vieja because it gives me a sense of comfort from home! Growing up in Miami, Florida, you can find Cuban food on almost every corner, even in gas stations. After serving in the military, I’ve discovered you can't find authentic Cuban food in nearly every state I've visited. Fortunately, my mom is Cuban, originally from Havana, and she's a fantastic cook! She’s just a phone call away if I need help with a Cuban recipe. But this one is by far my favorite! Ropa Vieja translates to “old clothes”; it’s named this because the beef shreds like old clothes. Ropa Vieja would impress anyone, even if they have never tried it; it makes a large quantity of food and is typically served over white rice with sides of black beans and platanos maduros (sweet plantains).

Recipe Details

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 6-10 hours on low

Total Time: 6-10 hours 20 minutes

Servings: 6

Ingredients

  • Sazón (to taste)
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 2 to 2.5 pounds beef flank steak
  • Salt, pepper, cumin (adjust to taste)
  • 2 large bay leaves
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bell peppers (red and green), sliced
  • 2 cups tomatoes or canned (cubed)
  • 1 can (8 ounces) tomato sauce or (2 ounces of tomato paste)
  • 1 sprig thyme
  • ½ cup cooking Sherry
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1-2 cups beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon dried parsley
  • Extras: carrots, green olives, and green peas to taste.
  • Garnish: Pimentos and/or fresh parsley for serving.

Instructions

  1. Trim the excess beef fat, then season with salt, pepper, cumin, and Sazón. Adjust to your liking.
  2. Heat about two tablespoons of oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the beef in a single layer and sear on both sides until browned (about 3-4 minutes per side). After browning one side, add the bay leaves.
  3. Add the remaining oil to the skillet over medium-high heat. Then add the onions, garlic, bell peppers, tomatoes, tomato sauce, thyme, paprika, cumin, Sazón, and any other extras you’d like. Cook for about 3-5 minutes.
  4. Add broth, turn off the heat, and transfer all ingredients to the slow cooker.
  5. Slow cook until the beef is tender (about 4 to 5 hours on high or 6 to 10 hours on low). Shred the beef using two forks and set aside.
    6. .
  6. Adjust the seasoning with more Sazón if necessary, then add garnish when plating.
  7. Serve over rice, black beans, and platanos maduros.
